Some Fun Facts About Me

My second great-granduncle Haven Gillespie, born in 1888, wrote the Christmas classic Santa Claus is Coming to Town in around 1933. Gillespie famously wrote the lyrics on the back of an envelope, while traveling on the NY subway, in only 15 minutes. His songs went on to be performed by many of the greats including Bruce Springsteen, Duke Ellington, and Frank Sinatra. I'd like to say that I inherited some of my great-granduncle's musical talent, but I am less of a lyrical genius and more the type to enjoy the musical talents of others.

My family has always been very artistic, my parents ran a theatre company while I was growing up and my sibling went to NYU Tisch for acting.The first thirteen years of my life were spent in and around plays, from Shakespeare camps to a two year stint at Oakland School for the Arts, I thought acting was my future. Unfortunately, the pressure around looks, lines, and success was crushing for me, and the people of the theatre world are some of the most pompous I've ever met. Challenge

The challenging part of this lab was getting both the sidebar and main content to sit nicely together even on a small screen. At first the main content kept getting way too small and it took awhile to figure out what width and min-widths would give the desired effect.

Problems

The sticky position attribute seemed simple enough at first until it started disappearing halfway through scrolling down the page. Through searching the code and referencing W3 schools, it became apparent that it was being cut off by the size of its parent element. This was fixed by setting a height bigger than required for the content div.

Results

This lab resulted in an uploaded lab10 page styled using positioning such as sticky and relative as well as a navigational bar and sidebar. We now have a better understanding of how to position elements where we want them using several different positioning attributes. We also can now add sidebars and menus to enhance our pages.
