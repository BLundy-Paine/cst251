Subject of this Lab

This lab detailed the importance of file structure in HTML and the difference between files and folders/directories. By keeping an organized file structure you can easily refer to elements in other folders such as images and CSS stylesheets.

Difficulties Faced

While completing this lab my partner and I had difficulties remembering the conventions around naming and nesting tags and frequently had to "view in browser" to check if issues were resolved. One issue was around linking to an image in a file, by looking at the examples given in the powerpoint it became apparent that the "src" information had been mistyped, after correcting the error the issue was fixed.

Our Results

Our results show organized code that led to a functioning page with viewable images and clickable links. Through freecodecamp we figured out how to open links in a new tab by adding "target='_blank'" after the link.
